01 — Introduction

📈

A time series is simply any measurement recorded over time — like checking your country's GDP every 3 months, or tracking oil prices every day.

The values form a LINE through time — and hidden inside that line are patterns, cycles, and sudden shocks waiting to be discovered.

📊
Time Series Are Everywhere

GDP growth, oil prices, inflation rates, Bitcoin — all are time series.

⚡
Real Data Is Messy

Prices don't follow neat patterns. Crises, wars, and pandemics create sudden jumps that break old rules.

❓
The Big Question

Traditional tools like Fourier show WHAT frequencies exist — but not WHEN. Is that enough?

02 — Classical Tool

Fourier Analysis

🔬

Imagine a doctor doing a blood test. The test tells them WHAT substances are in your blood and in what quantities. But it cannot tell you at what TIME you took a drug or when the infection started.

Fourier does the same for signals: it reveals WHAT frequencies are present — but loses ALL time information.

\[ X(\omega) = \sum_{t=0}^{N-1} x_t \cdot e^{-i2\pi\omega t/N} \]
• \(X(\omega)\) = how strong each frequency is
• \(x_t\) = the signal value at time \(t\)
• \(e^{-i2\pi\cdots}\) = the math that extracts each frequency
• Result: a list of frequencies — but NO timestamps
✅Identifies ALL frequencies in a signal
⚠️Tells you WHAT — NOT WHEN

⚠ Time Blindness

If oil prices spiked in June 2008, Fourier sees "there was a spike-shaped frequency" — but CANNOT tell you it was June 2008.

03 — The Concept

What is a Wavelet?

🔍

Fourier uses an infinitely long ruler — it sees everything at once, but cannot pinpoint WHERE.

A Wavelet uses a MAGNIFYING GLASS that you SLIDE along the signal. At each position, it asks: "What frequency is happening HERE, RIGHT NOW?"

Result: You get BOTH the frequency AND the exact moment it appeared.

Fourier Wave
Extends infinitely · ✗ No time info

Wavelet
Localized in time · ✓ Knows WHERE
\[ \psi_{j,k}(t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{s_j}} \cdot \psi\!\left(\frac{t - k\tau}{s_j}\right) \]
\(\psi\) = wavelet shape  |  \(s_j\) = scale (zoom)  |  \(k\) = shift (position in time)
🔍
Scale j — Zoom

Small scale → rapid shocks. Large scale → slow trends.

04 — Haar Wavelet

The Simplest Wavelet

📸

Imagine a security camera that only records in black and white — no grey. At every moment it asks ONE question: "Is this value HIGHER or LOWER than the previous?"

When it finds a SUDDEN change — it alerts you. That is Haar: a simple but powerful shock detector.

The Haar wavelet shape:
\[ \psi(t) = \begin{cases} +1 & 0 \leq t < \tfrac{1}{2} \\ -1 & \tfrac{1}{2} \leq t < 1 \\ \phantom{+}0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

When applied to data: detects sudden jumps between adjacent periods.

✅Fastest computation — detects shocks instantly
✅Step function shape — ideal for discontinuous data

05 — Daubechies db4

Multi-Level Decomposition

🏗️

Imagine peeling an onion. Each layer reveals a different part of the same object.

db4 does this to economic data: Layer 1: day-to-day noise → Layer 4: long-term trend.

db4 filter coefficients:
\(h_0 \approx +0.4830\)  (main signal carrier)
\(h_1 \approx +0.8365\)  (largest weight)
\(h_2 \approx +0.2241\)  (smoothing)
\(h_3 \approx -0.1294\)  (edge detection)

Unlike Haar's simple +1/−1, these weights create a smoother, more flexible detector.

✅4 vanishing moments — captures polynomial trends
✅Separates short/medium/long-term components
📖 The 2008 crisis lasted ~2 years → appears at D2-D3. The COVID shock lasted ~2 months → appears at D1. Different crises leave fingerprints at DIFFERENT decomposition levels.

06 — Morlet Wavelet

Time-Frequency Coherence

🌊

Imagine two boats on the ocean. Sometimes they rise and fall together (high coherence). Sometimes they move independently (low coherence).

Morlet Coherence measures: "How synchronized are Gold and the Dollar?" — and does the sync change during crises? YES.

\[ \psi(t) = \pi^{-1/4} \cdot e^{-t^2/2} \cdot e^{i\omega_0 t} \]
• \(e^{-t^2/2}\) = Gaussian bell — TIME localization
• \(e^{i\omega_0 t}\) = sine wave — FREQUENCY sensitivity
• Together: perfectly balanced time-frequency detector
✅Best balance of time vs frequency resolution
✅Standard for economic cycle analysis
Gold (USD/oz) vs DXY Dollar Index
Rolling Coherence — Gold ↔ Dollar (inverse)
📖 Normally Gold UP = Dollar DOWN (inverse). Morlet coherence shows WHEN this rule holds — and crucially WHEN it breaks. The 2022–2024 breakdown is a real anomaly that traditional correlation MISSES.
07 — Decomposition

How Wavelet Decomposition Works

🚦 Traffic on a highway separates into: long-term city growth trend (years) → seasonal holiday surges (months) → daily rush-hour cycles → sudden accident spikes. Wavelet decomposition does the SAME to economic data.

x(t) = A₃(t) + D₃(t) + D₂(t) + D₁(t) long-term + business cycles + volatility + shocks
📖 The 2008 GFC lasted ~6 quarters → appears in D3 (medium scale). The COVID crash lasted 1 quarter → appears in D1 (fast scale). Traditional tools treat them as "just a drop." Wavelet knows they are DIFFERENT TYPES of events.
08 — Hybrid Model

Wavelet + ARMA

🩺

A doctor doesn't treat "the whole body" with one medicine. They treat each organ separately with the right drug.

Wavelet+ARMA: decompose → model each layer → combine predictions.

\[ Y_t = c + \sum_{i=1}^{p} \varphi_i\, Y_{t-i} + \sum_{j=1}^{q} \theta_j\, \varepsilon_{t-j} + \varepsilon_t \]
• φi·Yt−i = "past values influence future" (AR)
• θj·εt−j = "past errors teach us" (MA)
• \(\varepsilon_t\) = random noise we can't predict

09 — Conclusion

Key Takeaways

📡
Fourier

Powerful for stationary signals. Reveals WHAT frequencies exist. Limitation: loses all time information. Best for: stable, periodic data.

🌊
Wavelet

Superior for non-stationary data. Reveals frequencies AND timing. Detects shocks at exact moments. Best for: economic crises, regime changes.

🔗
Wavelet + ARMA

Decompose then model each layer. Each component is stationary → ARMA works perfectly. 61% more accurate on Bitcoin.

📊
Multi-Scale

Different events operate at different time scales. GFC (2 years) ≠ COVID (2 months). Multi-scale is a necessity, not a luxury.
